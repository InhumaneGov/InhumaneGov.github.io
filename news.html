The International Court of Justice (ICJ) has found it "plausible" that Israel's actions in Gaza could amount to genocide in a case brought by South Africa.

Key Evidence:

  • Widespread Killing and Harm: Over 34,000 Palestinians killed, with the majority being women and children.
  • Destruction of Essential Infrastructure: Over 60% of Gaza's homes damaged or destroyed.
  • Imposition of a Siege: Blockade creating famine and disease crisis.
  • Incitement by Officials: Dehumanizing rhetoric from some Israeli officials.

Russia's Invasion of Ukraine

Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine is a clear violation of international law and the UN Charter, which prohibits acts of aggression.

Key Evidence:

  • Violation of International Law: UN General Assembly passed a resolution demanding Russia withdraw (141 in favor).
  • Annexation of Territory: Illegal annexation of four Ukrainian regions following sham referendums.
  • Documented War Crimes: Willful killings, torture, sexual violence, and attacks on civilian infrastructure.

Cited Sources:

  • UN General Assembly Resolution

    Text and voting record of the resolution "Aggression against Ukraine".

  • ICC Arrest Warrants

    International Criminal Court arrest warrants for President Putin and others.

  • UN Commission of Inquiry Report

    Documenting Russian war crimes, including attacks on civilian targets.

US Violations of Constitutional Rights

While operating under a constitutional framework, the US faces criticism for ongoing violations of constitutionally protected rights.

Key Evidence:

  • Abortion Rights: Overturning of Roe v. Wade eliminating constitutional right to abortion.
  • Voting Rights: Laws creating barriers that disproportionately disenfranchise minority voters.
  • First Amendment and Protest Rights: Aggressive policing and legal charges against protestors.

Cited Sources:

  • Dobbs v. Jackson

    Supreme Court opinion overturning Roe v. Wade.

  • Brennan Center on Voting Laws

    Summary of recent state laws that restrict voting access.

  • ACLU on Protest Rights

    American Civil Liberties Union's stance on the right to protest.

Conclusion

The assertion that these governments are acting in an inhumane manner is supported by documented actions and legal findings from international courts, United Nations bodies, and human rights organizations. The evidence points to severe breaches of international humanitarian law, acts of aggression, and the erosion of fundamental rights, leading to immense human suffering across different regions of the world.
